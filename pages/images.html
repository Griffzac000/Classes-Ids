Streetwear: A casual, youth-driven style that blends comfort, bold graphics, and cultural influences from skateboarding, hip-hop, sportswear, and Japanese street culture.

Classical: This style emphasizes refinement, balance, and durability, drawing inspiration from traditional European tailoring and mid-20th-century fashion icons. Classical fashion aims to look sophisticated and put-together in any era.

Grunge: a rebellious, undone style that became popular in the early 1990s, inspired by the grunge music scene in places like Seattle. It focuses on looking effortless and slightly messy, often using thrifted or worn-looking clothing.

Formal: clothing worn for special or prestigious occasions, where the goal is to look polished, elegant, and sophisticated. It usually involves structured, well-fitted garments made from high-quality fabrics.

Casual: about comfort, simplicity, and everyday wear. It focuses on pieces that are easy to throw on while still looking put-together. Common items include T-shirts, jeans, hoodies, sweatshirts, leggings, shorts, sneakers, and simple accessories.
